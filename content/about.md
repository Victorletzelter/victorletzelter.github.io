Victor Letzelter is a former student from the M.Res [MVA](https://www.master-mva.com/) at ENS Paris-Saclay in **Mathematics and Machine Learning** and **Applied Mathematics Engineer** from [École des Mines de Saint-Étienne](https://www.mines-stetienne.fr/en/) (French "Grandes Écoles").

During his curriculum, Victor worked for [Ciemat](https://www.ciemat.es/) (Madrid, Spain) in the National Laboratory of Fusion and in the Machine Learning research team of [Neural Concept](https://www.neuralconcept.com/) (Lausanne, Switzerland). His experience in Madrid was focused to event detection in multivariate time-series, and the one in Neural Concept was related to Multi-task learning on Geometric Neural Networks. 

Victor is passionated about mathematics and its applications, in particular artificial intelligence (deep and reinforcement learning, perception, vision, sound) and random modeling (computational statistics, state-space models, control theory). His current research interests include frugal learning (with little annotated data available), and interpretable learning (via the incorporation of prior knowledge in deep learning models).
